PG  - 722-37
AB  - This review discusses the importance and properties of antimicrobial peptides from 
      frogs and their synthetic analogues as potential therapeutic alternatives in 
      fighting not only bacterial infections, but also protozoans involved with the major 
      neglected diseases, which afflict human populations (e.g., Chagas disease, African 
      sleeping sickness, Leishmaniasis and malaria). Here, we emphasize their 
      multifunctional properties such as promising broad-spectrum drugs that target 
      protozoan parasites too.
